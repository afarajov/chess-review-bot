A game review that explains

ChessLens is a Telegram bot and web interface for analyzing chess games. The engine finds the mistakes, and the AI coach explains them so you know what to work on next.

How the analysis works

Every position is evaluated by Stockfish (depth 16). The evaluation is converted into a win probability, and each move is graded by how much of that probability it gave away:

CategoryConditionMeaning
★ Best matches the engine's top line the strongest move in the position
Excellent loses ≤ 2% nearly as good as the best move
✓ Good loses ≤ 5% a sound, solid move
≡ Book known opening theory a standard, well-established move
□ Forced only reasonable move no real alternative in the position
?! Inaccuracy loses ≤ 10% part of the advantage slips away
? Mistake loses ≤ 20% seriously damages the position
✕ Miss missed opportunity failed to punish the opponent's mistake
?? Blunder loses > 20% changes the outcome of the game

FAQ

Is it free?

Yes, the basic review is free. Limits and a subscription will come later, once LLM commentary is live.

How is this different from Chess.com's Game Review?

It works with both platforms at once, lives in Telegram, and focuses on explanations — not just grades.

Do I need an account?

No — reviewing a game by link works without logging in. An account lets you link Chess.com/Lichess and keep your review history.

Can anyone see my games?

Games on Chess.com and Lichess are public anyway. We only store the analysis results for your history.
